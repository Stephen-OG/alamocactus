Psychiatric Evaluations

A psychiatric evaluation is an assessment conducted by a mental health professional to understand an individual’s mental health, establish a diagnosis, and develop a treatment plan.

Depression

A mood disorder that causes persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue, and feelings of hopelessness. It can affect sleep, appetite, and energy levels.

Anxiety Disorders

Includes generalized anxiety, panic attacks, and phobias. People feel intense fear or worry, often with physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat or shortness of breath.

Bipolar Disorder

A condition involving extreme mood swings between emotional highs (mania) and lows (depression), affecting behavior, sleep, and energy levels.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

Triggered by experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. Symptoms include flashbacks, nightmares, and severe anxiety.

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

Affects focus, impulse control, and organization. Common in children and adults, leading to difficulties in school, work, or relationships.

Schizophrenia

A serious mental illness that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. Symptoms may include hallucinations, delusions, and disorganized thinking.
